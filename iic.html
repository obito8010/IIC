About IIC

In the year 2018, the Ministry of Education (MoE) through MoE's Innovation Cell (MIC) launched the Institution's Innovation Council (IIC) program in collaboration with AICTE for Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs) to systematically foster the culture of innovation and start-up ecosystem in education institutions.

Primarily, IICs' role is to engage large number of faculty, students and staff in various innovation and entrepreneurship related activities such as ideation, Problem solving, Proof of Concept development, Design Thinking, IPR, project handling and management at Pre-incubation/Incubation stage, etc., so that innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystem gets established and stabilized in HEIs.

Primary Focus

The primary focus of an Institution's Innovation Council (IIC) is to foster a culture of innovation and entrepreneurship within higher education institutions (HEIs). This involves creating a vibrant local innovation ecosystem, supporting student startups, and preparing institutions for the Atal Ranking of Institutions on Innovation Achievements framework.

Annual Innovation Summit 2023

Event Information

Date: October 15-16, 2023

Time: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

Venue: University Innovation Center

Participants: 500+ innovators and industry leaders

Event Highlights

  • Keynote speeches from leading tech CEOs
  • Panel discussions on emerging technologies
  • Startup showcase and exhibition
  • Networking sessions with investors
  • Innovation awards ceremony

About the Event

The Annual Innovation Summit is our flagship event that brings together the brightest minds in academia, industry, and entrepreneurship. This year's theme was "Innovating for Sustainable Future," focusing on technologies and solutions that address global challenges while creating economic opportunities.

Attendees had the opportunity to learn from industry pioneers, participate in hands-on workshops, and connect with potential collaborators and investors. 24-Hour Hackathon 2023

Event Information

Date: August 7-8, 2023

Time: 10:00 AM - 10:00 AM (24 hours)

Venue: University Tech Hub

Participants: 150 students in 30 teams

Event Highlights

  • 24-hour coding marathon
  • Focus on solving real-world problems
  • Mentorship from industry professionals
  • $10,000 in prizes
  • Internship opportunities for winners

About the Event

The 24-Hour Hackathon challenged student teams to develop innovative solutions to pressing societal problems within a tight timeframe. This year's challenges focused on healthcare accessibility, sustainable transportation, and education technology.

Participants worked through the night, fueled by coffee, snacks, and their passion for innovation. The event culminated in presentations to a panel of judges from leading tech companies, with the winning team developing an AI-powered platform for early detection of learning disabilities in children.

Startup Pitch Competition 2023

Event Information

Date: June 12, 2023

Time: 2:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Venue: University Auditorium

Participants: 15 startups, 8 investors

Event Highlights

  • Pitch presentations to investors
  • Feedback from industry experts
  • Funding opportunities
  • Networking with venture capitalists

About the Event

The Startup Pitch Competition provided a platform for student entrepreneurs to present their innovative business ideas to a panel of experienced investors and industry experts. This event offered participants the unique opportunity to secure funding, gain valuable feedback, and establish connections within the startup ecosystem.

Participants had 10 minutes to pitch their ideas followed by a 5-minute Q&A session with the judges. The competition was intense, with startups showcasing solutions ranging from sustainable packaging to AI-driven healthcare diagnostics. The winning team secured $25,000 in seed funding and mentorship opportunities.

Design Thinking Workshop 2022

Event Information

Date: September 5-6, 2022

Time: 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM

Venue: Design Innovation Lab

Participants: 80 students and faculty

Event Highlights

  • Human-centered design principles
  • Ideation techniques
  • Prototyping methods
  • Case studies

About the Event

The Design Thinking Workshop introduced participants to the principles and methodologies of human-centered design. Through interactive sessions and hands-on activities, attendees learned how to approach complex problems with empathy, creativity, and innovation.

The workshop covered the entire design thinking process: empathizing with users, defining problems, ideating solutions, prototyping concepts, and testing ideas. Participants worked in teams to tackle real-world challenges, applying the techniques they learned to develop innovative solutions. By the end of the workshop, each team had developed a prototype and presented their solution to the group.

IP Rights Workshop 2023

Event Information

Date: March 18, 2023

Time: 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM

Venue: Law Department Seminar Hall

Participants: 120 innovators and researchers

Event Highlights

  • Types of intellectual property
  • Patent filing process
  • Case studies of successful patents
  • Q&A with IP experts

About the Event

The Intellectual Property Rights Workshop provided crucial knowledge about protecting innovations through proper intellectual property management. This workshop was designed for researchers, innovators, and entrepreneurs who wanted to understand how to safeguard their creative works and inventions.

Participants learned about different types of intellectual property including patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets. The session included practical guidance on the patent application process, strategies for commercializing intellectual property, and case studies of successful IP management from both academia and industry.
